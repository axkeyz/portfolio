    Thoughts on replacing my laptop

    I’ve been using a Macbook Air Mid-2013 from, well, 2013. It was my first high quality laptop, and one that was the state of art at the time. From the moment I got this laptop, I thought, I’m now married to my laptop.

    But as I grow up, I realise that…maybe I’ve just grown out of it. Maybe it’s because 4 GB RAM doesn’t do much apart from browsing the web. Maybe it’s because I want to run stuff like Blender and my laptop just can’t handle it. Maybe because so many programs run on Windows and this laptop runs them like a slug.

    As long as I compromise, I can still get away with running Godot or an art program or two. Got to cut the music in the background though, too taxing even if I use Firefox over Chrome.

    Well, it’s hard to say whether I love or hate my laptop.

    I think I’m still leaning towards like, but it’s almost time to get a new laptop.

    I can’t decide whether to go for a beefy business laptop or a gaming laptop. Gonna go straight for 16+ GB of RAM and maybe with an upgradeable SSD. A dedicated graphics card would be amazing, and the new AMD Ryzens look pretty great.

    Unfortunately, with Covid around, the underspec’d feels overpriced and a lot of the somewhat more reasonably better-spec’d devices are out of stock. It doesn’t help that in New Zealand, there aren’t that many options to choose from as well.

    Oh well, I might just have to compromise with using my laptop for another two years or so. Hopefully I’ll replace it in 2023.
