What math means to me

Presented by Aileen Huang

Childhood

Skipped on homework all the time
Rather go out for a climb
What was math I did not know?
Thought it could not be my foe

Sadly, I got caught one day
Gone was all my fun and play
Way too many slacker years
Had to start basics in tears

Soon it was not all that bad
Math was rather fun I'd add
My marks rose and got ahead
So good it got in my head

But Year Twelve was way too much
I began to lose my touch
Blamed it on my advanced class*
My heart long like broken glass

Coding

When I lost math I gained code
A force so great to behold
Sums were solved so easily
Offered answers breezily

Then I found the math was wrong
Answers came out way too long
Floating point math was not right*
Wrong was wrong however slight

It was not that hard to fix
Cool that math was in the mix
I plodded on with much fun
My math fears a bit undone

Family was not a fan
Laptop got hit with a ban
But I think they realised
My hunger was undisguised

Continuation

So what does math mean to me?
I don’t really know you see
I’ve loved it so much I cried
Hated it so much I tried*

From year thirteen I did none
It was my foe to outrun
It only made me want more
As if it was in my core

While I knew more in the past
Perhaps this time I will last
So I guess that in the end
I still wish math is my friend
